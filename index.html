MultiGPT: Multimedia Generative Pretrained Transformer

Yu Hao   Zhenhua Li   Yi Fang

Multimedia and Visual Computing Lab (MMVC), New York Unviersity, Abu Dhabi and New York

Abstract

Generative Pretrained Transformers (GPT) have revolutionized human-AI interaction through natural language processing. However, the limitations posed by solely relying on textual channels hinder the potential for richer and more immersive information exchange between AI and humans. The MultiGPT project addresses this gap by developing a multifunctional GPT model capable of processing and generating diverse multimedia inputs and outputs, thus significantly enriching the interaction between humans and AI systems. Utilizing advanced deep learning techniques, MultiGPT seamlessly processes various multimedia inputs, such as images, videos, audio, and text, and generates corresponding multimedia outputs. This ongoing project is a collaboration between researchers from the Multimedia Visual Computing group at New York University Abu Dhabi and the New York campus. MultiGPT demonstrates substantial improvements in multimedia understanding and generation, fostering more intuitive and immersive human-AI interactions. As the model continues to evolve and expand its support for diverse multimedia content, MultiGPT is poised to transform various industries, becoming a vital component in next-generation AI systems.

Approach

DTI framework

Deep transformation module

Given a sample and prototypes and , standard clustering such as K-means assigns the sample to the closest prototype. Our DTI clustering first aligns prototypes to the sample using a family of parametric transformations - here rotations - then picks the prototype whose alignment yields the smallest distance.

We predict alignment with deep learning. Given an image , each deep parameter predictor predicts parameters for a sequence of transformations - here affine, morphological and thin plate spline transformations - to align the prototype to the query image .

Results

Standard image clustering benchmarks

MegaDepth locations

MegaDepth Florence: detailed results

We show the 6 best qualitatives prototypes learned using DTI clustering with 20 clusters for Florence location in MegaDepth dataset. For each cluster, we show the 20 samples leading to minimal reconstruction errors among all the samples in the cluster as well as corresponding transformed prototypes. Note how it manages to model real image transformations like illumination variations and viewpoint changes.

Instagram hashtags

We show the 5 best qualitatives prototypes learned using DTI clustering with 40 clusters for different Instagram photo collections. Each collection corresponds to a large unfiltered set of Instagram images (from 10k to 15k) associated to a particular hashtag. Identifying visual trends or iconic poses in this case is very challenging as most of the images are noise. You can visualize the type of collected images directly in Instagram: #balitemple, #santaphoto, #trevifountain, #weddingkiss, #yogahandstand.
